# Course Description

This intensive two-week block course offered at King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST), that provides a comprehensive foundation in the principles and applications of infectious disease epidemiology, with a strong emphasis on microbial genomics, bioinformatics, and statistical analysis. Designed for students and professionals in microbiology, public health, and computational biology, the course is structured with **online lectures in Week 1** and **in-person practical sessions in Week 2**.

---

# Course Overview

## Week 1: Online Lectures

Week 1 introduces core concepts in infectious disease epidemiology, microbial genomics, and antimicrobial resistance through online lectures. Participants will learn key definitions, methods for calculating epidemiological measures such as risk and rates, and the fundamentals of cohort and case-control studies.

This week will also cover analytical epidemiology, molecular tools for outbreak detection and surveillance, antimicrobial resistance, seroepidemiology, contact pattern studies, and vaccine epidemiology.

A guest lecture by **Dr. Abrar Alasmari from the Ministry of Health (MOH)** will take place on **18 June 2026**.

## Week 2: In-Person Practicals

Week 2 focuses on in-person, hands-on practical training in microbial genomics, bioinformatics, phylogenetics, and outbreak analysis.

Participants will work with real datasets and bioinformatics tools, including **R, Linux, high-performance computing (HPC), Artemis**, and workflows for genome assembly, mapping, variant calling, phylogenetic analysis, plasmidome analysis, resistance gene tracking, and outbreak investigation.

The final days include applied analysis sessions, a journal club, and participant presentations.

---

# Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, participants will be able to:

- Define and apply key concepts in infectious disease epidemiology, including risk measurement, study design, and bias.
- Perform microbial genome analysis, including assembly, variant calling, and phylogenetic reconstruction using command-line tools and graphical platforms.
- Analyze antimicrobial resistance mechanisms and interpret their genomic context through plasmidome profiling and comparative genomics.
- Conduct outbreak investigations using molecular epidemiology tools and phylogenetic approaches.
- Critically evaluate scientific literature through structured journal club discussions.
- Synthesize and present research findings effectively to peers through oral presentations and visual data displays.

---

# Delivery Format

- **Week 1:** Online lectures led by experts in epidemiology, infectious diseases, genomics, and public health.
- **Week 2:** In-person practical sessions using real datasets, R, Linux, and high-performance computing (HPC) environments.

---

# Not Covered in the Course

The following topics are explicitly outside the scope of this course:

- Clinical or environmental microbiology  
- Immunology  
- Infectious disease modeling  
- General microbiology theory  

---

# Target Audience

Graduate students with an interest in microbiology, epidemiology, and bioinformatics.

---

# Assessment Method

## Summative Assessment (100%)

- **Written Exam (30%)**  
  Closed-book exam assessing understanding of core concepts from the lectures.

- **Oral Presentation (70%)**  
  Each student will deliver a short presentation on a selected pathogen, discussing its epidemiology, virulence factors, and antimicrobial resistance on the last day of the course.  
  Pathogens are assigned on Day 1. This assessment emphasizes critical thinking, synthesis of literature, and clear scientific communication.

## Formative Assessment (Not Graded)

- **Online Quizzes (Week 1 & Week 2)**  
  Optional multiple-choice quizzes released at the end of each week for self-evaluation and reinforcement of key concepts.
