    Course Description¶

    This intensive two-week block course provides a comprehensive foundation in the principles and applications of infectious disease epidemiology, with a strong emphasis on microbial genomics, bioinformatics, and statistical analysis. Designed for students and professionals in microbiology, public health, and computational biology, the course blends theoretical lectures with practical hands-on sessions.

    Course Overview¶

    Week 1¶

    Week 1 introduces core concepts in infectious disease epidemiology, microbial genomics, and antimicrobial resistance. Participants learn key definitions, methods for calculating epidemiological measures such as risk and rates, and the fundamentals of cohort and case-control studies.

    The practical component introduces bioinformatics tools including R, Linux, high-performance computing (HPC) usage, Artemis, and workflows for genome assembly, mapping, variant calling, and phylogenetic analysis.

    Week 2¶

    Week 2 advances into analytical epidemiology, multivariate and survival analysis, and molecular tools for outbreak detection and surveillance. Students explore tools such as Outbreak-BEAST and BEAST for Bayesian phylogenetic inference, along with methods for plasmidome analysis and resistance gene tracking.

    The final days include advanced topics such as seroepidemiology, contact pattern studies, and vaccine epidemiology, concluding with a journal club and participant presentations.

    Learning Outcomes¶

    By the end of this course, participants will be able to:

    • Define and apply key concepts in infectious disease epidemiology, including risk measurement, study design, and bias.
    • Perform microbial genome analysis, including assembly, variant calling, and phylogenetic reconstruction using command-line tools and graphical platforms.
    • Analyze antimicrobial resistance mechanisms and interpret their genomic context through plasmidome profiling and comparative genomics.
    • Conduct outbreak investigations using molecular epidemiology tools such as BEAST and Outbreak-BEAST.
    • Critically evaluate scientific literature through structured journal club discussions.
    • Synthesize and present research findings effectively to peers through oral presentations and visual data displays.

    Delivery Format¶

    • Morning sessions: Lectures led by experts in epidemiology and genomics.
    • Afternoon sessions: Hands-on training with real datasets using R, Linux, and high-performance computing (HPC) environments. Applied learning through data analysis and practice.

    Not Covered in the Course¶

    The following topics are explicitly outside the scope of this course:

    • Clinical or environmental microbiology
    • Immunology
    • Infectious disease modeling
    • General microbiology theory

    Target Audience¶

    Graduate students with an interest in microbiology, epidemiology, and bioinformatics.

    Assessment Method¶

    Summative Assessment (100%)¶

    • Written Exam (30%)
      Closed-book exam assessing understanding of core concepts from the lectures.

    • Oral Presentation (70%)
      Each student will deliver a short presentation on a selected pathogen, discussing its epidemiology, virulence factors, and antimicrobial resistance on the last day of the course.
      Pathogens are assigned on Day 1. This assessment emphasizes critical thinking, synthesis of literature, and clear scientific communication.

    Formative Assessment (Not Graded)¶

    • Online Quizzes (Week 1 & Week 2)
      Optional multiple-choice quizzes released at the end of each week for self-evaluation and reinforcement of key concepts.
